Terms of Service | Violating Computer Crime Law:

06 Apr 2013 |

I just received an email today from eff.org, stating that a new computer crime law would make violating hte terms of service on any given website, punishible with a minimum of five years in prison (up to life) and numerous exoberant fines. To give you an idea, eff.org gives several ridiculous examples.

  • Lying about your age on Facebook or posting anything misleading. This includes saying your married when you aren't, or that you are 5lbs. thinner than you truely are. Saying you live in a different city for safety reasons could land you in the big house as well.
  • Buying "Twilight" or any other "occult materials" through Square. Including Lotto tickets, and membership clubs.
  • Posting anything impolite on te New York Times web site.
  • Accessing your Google+ page through another service (Hootsuite etc.).
  • Saying your "tall, dark and handsome" on Craigslist can also land you with prison sentence for false or fraudulent content".
  • It also makes it illegal to access computers with authorization.This includes the terms of use policy at your local library and your employer.
  • And, to report security breaches of a companies website to the company.
  • Let's not forget deleting cookies from your computer.
  • Oh, or changing you ip address. etc.

These would all be catagorized as felonies.

Honestly, all this proves to me, is that social networks are no longer in the interests of the people they serve. If you want to protect yourself from this ridiculous law, known as the "Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA)", also known as Aaron's Law. Head over to eff.org to learn more.

eff.org also goes on to say that the law "...will debate how the law should treat users who work their way around technical measures aimed at identification, tracking, or preventing interoperability with other programs or services. " Voting on this bill happens on the 10th of this month.
