Bio

A few years ago, I was like everyone else. I went to college, searched for a job, and went dizzy trying to figure out what I wanted to do with my life. Then, I ended up in debt up to my eyeballs and had to drop college because of some fancy new laws that were passed to keep out those who don't have a high school education. I went from a 4.0 GPA to sitting at home wondering what to do. After wrapping my mind around the subject and looking at every single angle, I decided to stop. Stop thinking about it, stop drowning in worry, just fix things and use that new found time for some self discovery.

Fast forward to 2013, I'm a writer, I've done mixed media canvases for fund raising at Raken Jordan (the hospital), and I've learned a lot about life and computers. I've found out that my main loves are writing, anthropology, and programming, something I never knew about myself. I'm working on becoming literate in web security, privacy, programming and working on the command line.

If you come across me in real life (not likely), you'll see I don't like to talk. I'm a listener. If I'm in public, you might see me coding at the coffee shop, hagling fees with a librarian, walking my dog, or riding my beach cruiser. It's just as likely that I'll be reading a book or getting into an argument about strange topics. I can't carry on a light conversation, be warned, I love details.

Where I'm Living

Right now, I'm in Missouri. I'm staying in a two bedroom house with my parents who had to move down here because my brother recently became paralyzed. I stay in the attic most of the time, with my border collie, typing on the keys and learning new things. Right now those things include PGP, JavaScript, and Vim.
